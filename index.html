Sri Lanka – Kenya Bilateral Relations

Diplomatic relations between Sri Lanka and Kenya dates back to the year 1970. Both countries are members of the Commonwealth, the Non-Aligned Movement (NAM), IORA and G77. The High Commission is accredited, Uganda, Rwanda, Tanzania, Republic of Congo and the Democratic Republic of Congo. There are approximately 400-500 Sri Lankans living and working in Kenya.

At the political and diplomatic level, Sri Lanka and Kenya have been maintaining close and cordial links over the years. The two countries are rooted by shared values, aspirations, and common interest to overcome the challenges of development. There have been bilateral visits at the highest levels.

Consular

Registration of Marriage

In order to have a Marriage Registered at the High Commission, one of the parties to the marriage should be a Sri Lankan Citizen and should give a Notice of Marriage to the High Commission

Registration of Death

The Sri Lanka High Commission will register the death of Sri Lankan nationals and issue Death Certificates provided following conditions are satisfied: The deceased should have been a citizen of Sri Lanka.

Registration of Birth

The registration of birth of a child under the Births and Deaths Registration Ordinance must under normal circumstances be made within three months of the birth.

Police Clearance of Certificate

The Police Clearance Certificate Application should be forwarded, together with the following documents: -Photocopies of the data page, visa, endorsement and alteration pages of the applicants Passport -Application processing fee US$10/- per application.

Authentication of Documents

Applicant should submit to the Sri Lanka High Commission in Kenya a letter issued by the Department of Motor Traffic of Sri Lanka certifying the genuineness of the Sri Lanka Driving License issued to the applicant, with the authentication of the Consular Division of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Sri Lanka.

Importation of Animals

An application form can be downloaded by visiting their website: www.daph.gov.lk. The duly filled application along with the vaccination record for the pet should be faxed/ emailed or submit online to the Director General of the DAPH. Permission to import will be granted within a few weeks.
